Semarang

Semarang is a coastal city in Central Java, Indonesia. It blends historical areas like Kota Lama with modern urban life.

Quick Facts

Semarang is the capital of Central Java province, located on the northern coast of Java island. It's a bustling port city with a rich history, serving as a major trade hub since the Dutch colonial era. The city is famous for its multicultural heritage, with a blend of Javanese, Chinese, and Dutch influences evident in its architecture, cuisine, and culture. Iconic landmarks like Lawang Sewu and the Old Town district (Kota Lama) showcase its colonial past, while places like Sam Poo Kong Temple highlight its strong Chinese heritage. Semarang is also known for its local spring rolls, called lumpia, a popular specialty.
